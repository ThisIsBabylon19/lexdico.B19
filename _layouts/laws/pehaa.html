PEHAA: Prevention of Educational Harms and Administrative Abuse

Strengthening Statutory Penalties for Academic Malpractice

Students enrolled at higher education institutions in South Africa are unable to obtain timely refunds when programs are misrepresented and altered without consent. The ambiguity in legislation causes administrative abuse whereby institutions like Richfield have normalized a world of fraudulent falsifications in official documentation. There is a need for explicit statutory penalties against such academic malpractice, particularly when such fraudulent falsifications are used to unlawfully obstruct the release of a legitimate refund. South Africa enables bad-faith misconduct and needs clear statutory deterrence, stronger civil penalties and defined legal sanctions.
